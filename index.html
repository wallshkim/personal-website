About Me

I firmly believe that anyone can learn programming languages and frameworks if they are both passionate and intrinsically motivated.

Indeed, in twenty short weeks, I’ve gone from learning what a variable is, to building full-stack applications that respond to clients’ real world business problems.

However, as Bill Watterson once said, “Your preparation for the real world is not in the answers you’ve learned, but in the questions you’ve learned how to ask yourself,” and this is where I’m different.

My background in psychology and diverse experiences have led me to be introspective and taught me to be comfortable not just in the answers but in the questions that precede and follow them. This is what brought me to software engineering where technology is constantly evolving and where finding solutions requires embracing uncertainty.
